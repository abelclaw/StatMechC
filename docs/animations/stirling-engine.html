A low-temperature differential (LTD) Stirling engine runs on temperature differences as small as 5 °C. The large, thin displacer shuttles air between the warm bottom plate (heated by coffee) and the cool top plate (exposed to room air). The resulting pressure oscillation drives the small power piston, which turns the flywheel. The engine sits directly on the rim of the cup.
